PERMANENT MAKE-UP: ADVANTAGES, DISADVANTAGES AND RISKS

Permanent make-up has more advantages than disadvantages. It is the best solution for looking well 24 hours a day. It gives you the peace of mind of knowing that your make-up is always in place and that it is done in the way that best suits your face features and the color of your eyes, skin and hair, making you look attractive at any time. It also frees you forever from applying traditional cosmetic products on your face, such us lipsticks and eyeliners that might end up affecting your skin in the long run. It is of crucial importance that the esthetician is very well experienced in permanent makeup and that he or she has an extensive base of satisfied customers. Preferably, it would be good to know that among her customers there are people that you know, who can provide a referral.

The errors most commonly seen in the practice of permanent make-up are:

1) An inadequate outlining 2) Inadequate choosing of colors. These errors can be corrected but it demands some time and difficulty. Erasing a tattoo is much more difficult and expensive than making it. At the moment, there are some laser-based products and procedures for removing tattoos. These procedures are used only in extreme situations where the tattoo has to be completely removed and redone. However, in most cases, a simpler method is used that consists of retouching the old tattoo with new colors, applying some camouflage tones in the areas that need to be faded or eliminated.

About risk of contagion:

Absolutely all the materials used in permanent make-up are disposable. The tattooing machine uses needles, which are removed from their envelopes and installed in the machine before you; once your permanent make-up is done, all these envelopes are removed from the machine and properly disposed of.

Rejection to the pigments as rejection to foreign matter:

There is an extremely small possibility that one person’s organism rejects the pigmentation as a foreign matter. If you know of any allergies that you might have, you should inform about it to the esthetician; she can make a test in a small area, before doing the actual make-up job. People with tendency to anomalous scarring are somewhat unpredictable in regard to permanent make-up. In most cases, they do not have any problems. However, if you know that you have had this in the past, you should inform about this to the esthetician, as well.

The esthetician is not responsible for the patient`s rejection to foreign matter or anomalous scarring.

The esthetician’s job is limited to guarantee that the makeup is well designed and appropriate for your facial features and that every material used in the job is disposable.

This warranty represents about a 90% of the success of the permanent makeup, the rest being the patient’s responsibility in taking care of herself at home.

AFTERCARE INSTRUCTIONS FOR YOUR PERMANENT MAKE-UP

Please follow these recommendations after your PERMANENT MAKE-UP procedures of EYEBROWS, EYELINERS, LIPS, SHADE, AREOLAS, CAMUFLAGES or CAPILLARY MICROPIGMENTACION.

Ice the area consistently during the first 24 hours after the procedure by applying ice on it every 15 minutes. You should rest another 5 minutes after icing, then ice again. Put the ice inside a plastic bag and then wrap it around a towel so it is not so uncomfortable. By icing the area, you avoid swelling and the skin heals quicker.

You MUST apply cream (Vaseline, Vita E or Aquaphor) during the first two weeks after the procedure at least three times a day, or even better every two hours to hydrate the recently pigmented area. Wash or clean the area with wipes before applying the cream a second time. Not doing so will allow thick scabs to form, which will peel the color when they come off, not to mention the increase in risk of infection bellow the scab.

AVOID taking in direct sunlight during the first two weeks. Wear a hat and sunglasses when going out.

AVOID saunas, hot tubs or pools *chloride* during the first two weeks

Try NOT to shower for prolonged periods of time and avoid using very hot water. You can protect the area by applying some Vaseline before showering.

Do NOT use retinoic or any glycolic acid on pigmented areas while healing.

SPECIAL CARE INSTRUCTIONS FOR:

EYEBROWS: The pigmented area will look darker than expected during the first few weeks after the procedure. Don’t be alarmed if the little scabs fall off with some pigmentation; this is completely normal and will happen during the first few days, and the color will lighten after the little scabs have completely fallen off.

EYES: As well as the previous instructions, do not apply mascara or makeup while using the hydrating ointment (Vaseline, Vitamin E or Aquaphor); the eyeliners will look progressively thinner after the first few weeks. Do not apply antibiotics. Avoid eating high sodium foods; these may increase swelling. Do not use contact lenses on the first day and when going out, use sunglasses.

LIPS: Do not wet your lips with your tongue as this may increase the risk of lip herpes. If you know you carry the virus, please consult your physician.

You must also use alcohol-free mouthwash and avoid consuming hot, greasy, spicy or very salty foods.

Apply chopstick to moisten your lips. Your lips will feel very dry after the scabs have fallen off.

CUIDADOS POSTERIORES PARA EL MAQUILLAJE PERMANENTE

Después de realizado el maquillaje permanente de CEJAS, DELINEADO DE OJOS, LABIOS, SOMBRAS, AREOLAS, O CAMUFLAJES, se debe tener en cuenta las siguientes recomendaciones:

Mantener la zona fría durante las primeras 24 horas poniendo hielo 15 minutos y descansando 5 minutos). El hielo debe estar en una bolsa de plástico y envuelto en una toalla de papel. De esta manera se evita la inflamación y la piel sana pronto.

Aplicar crema (vaselina, vitamina E o Aquaphor) tres veces al día durante dos semanas. Entre cada aplicación, limpiar la crema anterior utilizando un papel suave o un Q-Tip. Si prefiere, puede lavar la zona con agua.

EVITE exponerse al sol durante las dos primeras semanas. Debe protegerse con un sombrero y gafas.

EVITE sauna, jacuzzis o piscina (agua con cloro) durante dos semanas. EVITE baño muy prolongado y el agua muy caliente. Puede proteger la zona poniendo un poco de vaselina antes del baño.

EVITE arrancar las cáscaras, si se forman. Si lo hace, el pigmento que está en proceso de fijación se caerá y quedarán espacios blancos. EVITE usar productos despigmentadores tales como ácido retinóico o glicólico cerca de las zonas recién maquilladas.

CUIDADOS ESPECIALES PARA: CEJAS: Además de los cuidados anteriores tenga en cuenta que el color se verá muy oscuro al principio. Es normal que se caigan pedacitos de cáscaras con color durante las dos primeras semanas. El color se verá más suave después de ese tiempo.

OJOS: Además de los cuidados anteriores, NO maquille las pestañas durante el tiempo que esté usando la crema humectante. Vaselina o vitamina E. Los delineados se verán más delgados después de dos semanas. NO use cremas antibióticas. NO consuma alimentos altos en sodio porque puede incrementar la inflamación. NO use lentes de contacto durante el primer día. Use gafas.

LABIOS: Además de los cuidados anteriores, NO humedezca los labios con saliva. Puede favorecer la aparición de herpes simple labial, (si usted es portadora del virus Herpes simple labial debe tener atención médica). Debe usar enjuague bucal SIN alcohol y debe evitar las comidas calientes, grasosas, picantes o muy saladas. Aplicar humectante de labios porque puede sentirlos muy resecos después de que pierda la cáscara.
